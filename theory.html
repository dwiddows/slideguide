The Harmonic Series

← Trombone Positions Calculator

Lips can buzz at pretty much any frequency, but a tube of a fixed length only supports standing waves at particular frequencies -- its harmonic series. Leave the trombone slide all the way in and buzz harder, and the note jumps from one partial of that series to the next: pedal B♭, then B♭, F, B♭, D, F, A♭ (a well-known flat one, not quite in tune), then B♭ again, an octave and a half above where it started.

Moving the slide out to a lower position lengthens the tube, and lowers the whole series with it -- try a different position below.

A plain tube: each partial fits n loops between two ends.

A tube that flares into a bell at the open end: the same n loops, but reshaped -- compressed near the narrow throat, stretched out near the bell.
